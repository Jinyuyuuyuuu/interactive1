Florence overview

Florence is one of the world most famous art capital in central Italy. Here is the birthplace of the European Renaissance.
Therefore, there are 40 museums and art galleries, more than 60 palaces, and numerous churches.
Besides, one of the five cathedrals of the world––Cattedrale di Santa Maria del Fiore (in English “Cathedral of Saint Mary of the Flower” usually called Florence Cathedral )
and world’s first art academy––Academy of Fine arts of Florence are also located here.

The weather in Florence is similar to San Francisco, just a little bit hotter.Summer is cool and winter is also not too cold.
Therefore, many families in Florence do not have air conditioning.
However, this year because of the global warming, the temperature in early June reached 40 degrees Celsius (104℉)!

Academy of Fine arts of Florence


The Florentine Academy of Fine Arts is a product of the Renaissance and has had a tremendous impact on the Renaissance.
The significance of this school is already not only a school. This school has existed as a witness to the development of
world civilization and a landmark in art and education. The Academy of Fine arts of Florence has cultivated countless masters of painting and sculpture
since its founding, among which are Leonardo Da Vinci, Michelangelo, Dante and Raffaele Santi...
In addition, the world well-known statue of David from Michelangelo is also collected in the art gallery of the Academy of Fine Arts in Florence.

Flowing Chocolate Wall

Everyone knows Italy has the best handmade ice cream in the world. This store was founded by a famous chocolate brand called “Venchi” and
it has a lot of chain store which located in many cities in Italy. If you come to Italy, I highly recommend you to visit here!
In this store, you can not only taste one of the most delicious ice cream, but also see a marvelous and mouthwatering flowing chocolate wall!

Unnamed Church


After visiting most of Florence’s famous scenery, we drove to an anonymous church in the suburbs with an Italian pastor.
That must be the most memorable part of the trip. When we walked out of the chapel,
we witnessed the sunlight was reflected directly onto Jesus, which the pastor called “God’s beam of light”.
